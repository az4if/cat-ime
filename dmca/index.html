DMCA & Copyright Policy

Cat-ime respects intellectual property rights. This page explains how we handle copyright concerns and where to send notices.

What Cat-ime is

Cat-ime is a free, client-side anime hub for discovery, lists, and progress tracking. We use the AniList API for titles, artwork, and metadata.

We do not host, upload, store, or distribute video files. Playback in the app is provided through embedded third-party players (for example, Vidnest). Those services operate independently and are responsible for the streams they serve.

Copyright complaints about video streams

If you believe a specific episode or stream accessible through the embed player infringes your copyright, the fastest path is to contact the embed provider directly, since they control the hosted content.

Contact Vidnest

Include the AniList ID, series title, episode number, and any URLs shown in the player when you write to them.

Notices about Cat-ime itself

For issues limited to this website (for example, metadata displayed on Cat-ime, account data, or other non-video content), you may contact us at thecatime@gmail.com.

Please include the information listed below so we can review your request.

Submit a copyright complaint

To report infringement, your notice should include:

  • Your full name and contact information (email and, if possible, phone number).
  • A description of the copyrighted work you believe has been infringed.
  • The exact URL or location of the material on Cat-ime (or the series/episode details shown in the app).
  • A statement that you have a good faith belief the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  • A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on the owner's behalf.
  • Your physical or electronic signature.

Send notices regarding Cat-ime to thecatime@gmail.com. We will review valid notices and respond as appropriate.

Counter-notification

If you believe material was removed or disabled in error, you may send a counter-notice that includes:

  • Your name, address, and contact details.
  • Identification of the material that was removed and where it appeared before removal.
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that the material was removed due to mistake or misidentification.
  • Your consent to the jurisdiction of the courts in your district (or where you are located), and that you will accept service of process from the person who filed the original notice.
  • Your electronic or physical signature.

Send counter-notices to thecatime@gmail.com.

Repeat infringers

Where applicable, we may limit access for users who are repeatedly determined to infringe copyrights after appropriate notice and review.
